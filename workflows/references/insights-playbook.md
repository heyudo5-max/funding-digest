# Step 2 — Gather Insights

Metrics tell you *what* happened. Insights tell a founder *what to do with it*. The aim is 3–6 insights that are **specific, non-obvious, and backed by the data you captured**. Each insight needs:

- **The claim** — one clear sentence.
- **Rationale (1–2 points)** — the specific data or examples that support it.
- **Source(s)** — where it came from (deal names, outlets, the metrics summary).
- **So-what for founders** — why someone building or raising should care.

Skip anything you can't support. A confident but ungrounded "AI is hot" is worse than three smaller claims you can actually defend.

## Prompt questions to mine

Use these as lenses. Not all will yield something every period — pick the ones the data actually supports.

1. **Beyond the obvious "hot" categories, how are investors behaving?**
   Look past "AI raised a lot." Are they favoring revenue-generating / capital-efficient companies? Concentrating dollars in fewer, bigger rounds? Backing applied/vertical AI over foundation models? Returning to "unsexy" sectors (manufacturing, logistics, defense, energy)? Rationale = stage/dollar concentration, sector tallies, specific rounds.

2. **Is there still momentum at early stage (pre-seed / seed)?**
   Use the count-share vs. dollar-share split. Lots of seed *deals* but shrinking seed *dollars* tells a different story than the reverse. Compare to the prior period. Founders raising early care a lot about this.

3. **What roles are funded companies hiring for?**
   After a raise, companies post roles — a leading signal of where they're investing. Check the funded companies' careers pages / LinkedIn for patterns (heavy GTM/sales = scaling revenue; heavy ML/research = product bet; ops/forward-deployed = enterprise push). Even a directional read ("post-raise, several are loading up on go-to-market, not just engineers") is useful.

4. **Founder profile trends — solo vs. team, first-time vs. repeat?**
   Are funded founders repeat operators? Ex-FAANG / ex-unicorn? Technical solo founders? Domain experts from the industry they're disrupting? This signals what investors are pattern-matching on. Source from announcement bios and LinkedIn.

5. **Other angles worth checking:**
   - **Geography shifts** — a region punching above its usual weight.
   - **Round structure** — bridge/extension rounds, flat/down rounds, large seeds acting like Series A.
   - **Investor identity** — are the same few funds leading? Any notable new or returning names? Crossover/strategic investors entering early?
   - **Acquisitions** — who's buying, at what apparent multiples, and what does it say about consolidation in a category?
   - **Time-to-raise / valuation chatter** — when sources mention it.
   - **Anti-trends** — a once-hot category going quiet is itself an insight.

## Quality bar

- **Specific beats broad.** "Three of this week's five largest rounds were applied-AI in regulated industries (legal, health, insurance)" beats "AI is popular."
- **Name names.** Concrete companies and investors make insights credible and watchable.
- **One period ≠ a trend.** For weekly digests, hedge appropriately ("worth watching"). For monthly+, you can be firmer because you're comparing periods.
- **Tie back to the founder.** End each insight with the practical read.

Output the insights as a short ranked list (most interesting first) before drafting the script — the strongest 2–3 become the spine of the video.
